Experiment boundary. The table below is not the earlier rule-based routing benchmark. 2026-04-20 audit note. Three earlier hard-target Qwen runs for HER2_AAV, BetV1, and CbAgo were started from the script default SEED_PROFILE=balanced and therefore did not test the intended routed target-specific seeds. Those canceled runs (7189792, 7189794, 7189796) also predated the mutation-surface fix that lets Qwen switch into sequence_hallucination and other human-designed Tier A program families directly. They should be treated as setup mistakes, not as benchmark evidence.

Corrected routed reruns were started on 2026-04-21 as 7199569 (HER2_AAV), 7199570 (BetV1), and 7199571 (CbAgo) with the expanded full mutation surface. This surface includes refinement.algorithm=sequence_hallucination and the associated refinement/search knobs, so the tree can move into the same broad program family as the local human-designed hallucination recipes.

9. How to Compare This Page to the Paper

Table 10

The most relevant repeated paper-hard targets here are CD45, BetV1, HER2_AAV, SC2RBD, and CbAgo.

Figure 18

Question: how do hallucination-family methods scale with GPU-hours on the paper’s hard targets?

Among the repeated targets emphasized on this page, SC2RBD, CD45, BetV1, HER2_AAV, and CbAgo belong here. SpCas9 and VEGFA are also paper Hard targets in Figure 18. SC2RBD belongs here, not in Figure 19.

Figure 19 / Sec. I.4

Question: what happens on the paper’s very-hard targets under long-horizon reward-shaped MCTS + G&H?

Only H1, TNFα, and IL17A should be compared directly to this section.

Comparison discipline. This page supports claims such as “better than our local baseline” or “directionally better than the paper’s zero generative anchor”. It does not support matched compute-scaling claims against Figure 18 or Figure 19 unless we rerun cumulative unique-success curves under paper-faithful accounting.

13. Benchmark Redesign Notes: Why the First 2-Hour Draft Was Canceled

A first 2-hour per target benchmark draft was launched and then canceled after review. The main issue was not the idea of a fixed-budget benchmark itself; it was that the first draft mixed a clean compute cap with branch settings and target assignments that were not yet aligned tightly enough with the audited evidence base.

Issue Found in the First Draft Why It Matters Required Fix Before Rerun
2h was a timeout cap, not guaranteed realized compute Some branches can finish well under 2 hours, while others naturally run much longer. The original draft therefore risked comparing different realized GPU-hours under the same timeout ceiling. Use measured realized GPU-hours in the comparison, or tune each branch so both control and routed arms reliably saturate the same budget.
Several routed branches were standardized drafts, not exact evidence-matched replays The current hallucination and mixed branches used greedy_percentage=5, even though the strongest local HER2_AAV and BetV1 evidence came from runs with the default greedy_percentage=1. The very-hard branch also standardized on rws5 despite target-specific local differences. Decide explicitly whether the benchmark tests exact audited replays or a standardized global program, then describe that choice consistently in both the figure and the launch script.
Some target assignments were transfer hypotheses CD45 -> noise, HER2_AAV -> hallucination, and SC2RBD -> MCTS have direct same-target support, but assignments such as DerF7-variant -> noise, HER2_AAV-variant -> hallucination, and CbAgo -> msg5 were extrapolations or negative controls. Either split the benchmark into same-target validated and transfer subsets, or keep the full panel but report those rows explicitly as transfer tests rather than as audited wins.
The initial launch is no longer part of the empirical record The submitted Exp 34 jobs were canceled before results were used, so the page should not imply that a clean benchmark has already been run. Record Exp 34 as a canceled draft benchmark, then relaunch only after the contract above is rewritten and checked.

What remains valid. The idea of a fixed-budget benchmark remains useful. The audited page now simply distinguishes the results record from a benchmark design that is treated separately.

Benchmark note. The initial Exp 34 launch was canceled after review. This page reports the benchmark only under an explicit benchmark contract, standardized branch definitions, and a clear transfer-vs-validated split.

A cleaner rerun would compare default control versus either (i) an exact audited replay program or (ii) a deliberately standardized global program, always reporting FoldSeek-unique successful self binders under matched realized compute.

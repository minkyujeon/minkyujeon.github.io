2. Paper Taxonomy: Easy / Hard / Very Hard

The Proteina-Complexa paper uses a clear split that this page now follows exactly. The main 19-target benchmark contains 12 Easy and 7 Hard targets. The paper then studies 3 Very Hard targets separately in Section I.4.

Paper Class Targets Where in the Paper How This Page Uses It
Easy IFNAR2, BHRF1, BBF14, DerF21, TrkA, PD1, Insulin, DerF7, PDL1, IL7RA, CrSAS6, Claudin1 Easy-target scaling and main 19-target benchmark Used only as the paper’s global benchmark class
Hard VEGFA, SpCas9, SC2RBD, CbAgo, CD45, BetV1, HER2-AAV Main benchmark; hard-target scaling includes Figure 18 Includes SC2RBD. It does not belong in Section I.4 / Figure 19 tables.
Very Hard H1, TNFα, IL17A Section I.4 and Figure 19 only These are the only targets that should be called Very Hard on this page.

Important correction. SC2RBD is a Hard target in the paper. It appears in Table 10 and the hard-target Figure 18 context. It is not a Section I.4 / Figure 19 Very Hard target.

3. Audited Local Results Map

The table below is the new core summary for repeated local targets. It is intentionally smaller than the previous "all 28 targets" table. The goal here is correctness: each row separates paper class, audited local baseline, and best verified local intervention.

Target Paper Class Audited Local Baseline Best Verified Local Intervention Current Interpretation
CD45 Hard Repeated base runs reproduce 8/32 self; redesign remains small but non-zero across reruns (5/32 and 7/32). noise=0.20 reaches 15/32 self; redesign stays near zero in the audited reruns. Local evidence is most consistent with a geometry-sensitive hard target. Higher generation noise helps; hallucination is not the first useful move.
HER2_AAV Hard Repeated base runs reproduce 1/32 self and 14/32 MPNN. Exp 4 hallucination reaches 21/32 self and 15/32 MPNN. Later Exp 27 schedule sweeps hit 16/16 self in short refinement-only runs. Strongest local sequence-sensitive case. The backbone often exists, but the original co-designed sequence is weak.
BetV1 Hard Repeated base runs reproduce 6/32 self; redesign is weak and slightly variable across reruns (0/32 to 2/32). Exp 13b noise+halluc reaches 11/32 self and 3/32 MPNN. Mixed hard target. It does not behave like CD45 or HER2_AAV cleanly; bundled interventions help more than single knobs.
SC2RBD Hard Audited local baseline remains 0/32 self and 0/32 MPNN. Exp 25 msg5 reaches 4/8 self and 0/8 MPNN. Exp 26 rw_pl050_softg5 reaches 5/8 self and 2/8 MPNN. Paper Hard target, local zero-hit baseline target. The clean local lesson is that beam-family baseline runs stay at zero, while MCTS plus the right refinement bundle opens the target.

Main empirical takeaway. The human-designed local campaign still supports the same high-level conclusion: with fixed model weights, inference-time choices matter a lot, but the right choice is target-specific.

Question: how do hallucination-family methods scale with GPU-hours on the paper’s hard targets?

Among the repeated targets emphasized on this page, SC2RBD, CD45, BetV1, HER2_AAV, and CbAgo belong here. SpCas9 and VEGFA are also paper Hard targets in Figure 18. SC2RBD belongs here, not in Figure 19.

Figure 19 / Sec. I.4

Question: what happens on the paper’s very-hard targets under long-horizon reward-shaped MCTS + G&H?

Only H1, TNFα, and IL17A should be compared directly to this section.

Comparison discipline. This page supports claims such as “better than our local baseline” or “directionally better than the paper’s zero generative anchor”. It does not support matched compute-scaling claims against Figure 18 or Figure 19 unless we rerun cumulative unique-success curves under paper-faithful accounting.

Autoresearch v2.2 — 48H multi-target campaign

Four 4-GPU 48-hour jobs on three targets (CD45, BetV1, SC2RBD) plus a calibration A/B on CD45. All use three_experts + PUCT + cap=2.0; within the CD45 A/B pair, the only intended variable is whether --disable-calibration-context is on. Snapshot generated 2026-05-06 from local Slurm outputs. Static, not auto-refreshing.

Current status (snapshot at this moment): CD45 cal-ON, BetV1, and SC2RBD have completed; CD45 cal-OFF is still running. Numbers below come from per-child eval reports plus a run-level global sequence dedupe audit, not from final best-child evaluation.

Don't over-read single-run differences. Same-config CD45 variance across our prior 24-hour runs is large (7606343 = 24 audited/global-unique vs 7619073 = 11 on identical setup, 2.7× spread). Differences smaller than ~10–15 audited successes between two runs are likely noise.

How to read this page

  • succ / GPU-h is the cleanest budget-normalized reporting metric here: run-level global-unique successes divided by total executed GPU-hours. Cross-target comparisons still include target difficulty, so the CD45 calibration A/B is the cleaner causal comparison.
  • min iPAE is the lowest interface PAE produced by any candidate the agent ran. This tells you whether the agent ever found a binder that could succeed, even if it didn't replicate.
  • Operator family bars show how many child runs the agent allocated to each search strategy. Diverse coverage is good; collapse to one family is a warning sign.
  • Cumulative global-unique successes show when in the run the discoveries happened — early bursts vs. late discoveries.

Methodology notes

  • Score is a per-child compute-normalized rate: audited_unique_success / gpu_hours, capped at 2.0, plus small soft bonuses for low iPAE / high pLDDT / low duplicate fraction. Cap exists to prevent a single fast small-denominator winner from anchoring the parent portfolio.
  • "Audited" successes are the AlphaFold2-evaluated unique candidates that meet iPAE ≤ 0.18 AND pLDDT ≥ 0.90, after de-duplication on sequence. "Online" successes use a softer per-fold criterion that the backend reports during sampling.
  • PUCT is the AlphaZero-style upper-confidence-bound parent selector. Q is the per-child score; visit count is the number of expansions per parent.
  • Calibration injection reads each role's predicted-vs-actual bias on best_i_pae / n_successful / expected_gpu_minutes accumulated so far, and adds it to the analyst's user prompt at iteration ≥ 5 with a "you have over-predicted X" warning. It is removed from the Refiner and Supervisor prompts (decision-safe) so calibration data does not leak into selection.
  • Failure modes include hard failures (timeout, OOM, backend exception) and soft failures (zero_success, near_miss_interface, duplicate_collapse, proxy_mismatch). Both kinds are surfaced to analysts via proposal_history in the EvidencePack.

Pipeline coherence check (verified directly from iter 10 of CD45 7694929)

The 3-expert / Refiner / Verifier / Supervisor stack is producing diverse and sensible output. Direct trace of one iteration:

RoleFindings (first claim)Suggested operator families
DomainExpertSearch stuck in a 'near-miss' local optimum: high pLDDT (> 0.93), insufficient interface precision (iPAE ~0.18-0.23). Sequence repeats KAL/ESE noted.fk-steering, mcts
MLExpertTrapped in a near-miss local optimum where increasing diversity or beam width alone is insufficient to escape the energy basin. Operator fatigue noted on diverse-beam-search.fk-steering, best-of-n
SystemExpertMetric definition mismatch: online success is 0 despite best_iPAE 0.187 being very close to the 0.18 threshold. High timeout risk on MCTS / FK-Steering.refinement, single-pass

The three experts produced different findings, different focuses, different operator suggestions. The Refiner then emitted three proposals each citing a specific prior child failure ("avoid timeout unlike child_019", "diverge from child_024 noise=0.2", "MCTS n_simulations=8 was insufficient + n_simulations=16 timed out — try 4 with higher exploration"). The Verifier rejected the fk-steering proposal because the Refiner hallucinated a manifest path (fk_steering.steering_strength doesn't exist) — the safety gate worked. The Supervisor selected best-of-n_high_noise; the controller actually launched both verifier-accepted proposals as multi-route children.

The pipeline design is coherent. The bottleneck is efficiency, not coherence: the agent reasoning is sensible but produces audited successes at a lower rate per sample than older v2 PUCT did.

Methodological comparison vs older v2 PUCT

The older v2 PUCT campaign (referenced in Section 7 of the legacy results page) reported 22/32 unique 5 on the best CD45 child. We get 5/132 (per the audit count) and 4/32 (per per-sample rate at root). The headline "older v2 looks better" comparison is misleading because:

The biggest single difference is the starting seed. Older v2 PUCT was effectively a "mutation refinement" task on top of a human-discovered good config. Our v2.2 starts from a generic difficulty-conditioned config (within 0.05 noise of noise020 but not target-tuned). Notably, our current 7694929 root child_000 (noise=0.25) produced 4 global-unique successes vs the older v2 root noise020 which the legacy page recorded at 1 unique — comparable starting point, similar or slightly better starting performance.

One observation about agent search direction: at iter 10 in 7694929, the agent proposed raising noise from 0.2 → 0.4 to "break the local optimum". The older v2 winning recipe stayed at noise=0.2. The agent tends to push noise upward when it could productively explore the lower-noise regime that human exploration originally found.

Each discovery is one new sequence from Complexa's success CSVs, cross-child deduplicated by binder_sequence. X axis = cumulative GPU-hours used in the run. Y axis = global-unique success count. Dots mark exactly when each new unique was found. The slope of the curve is what we want to maximize.

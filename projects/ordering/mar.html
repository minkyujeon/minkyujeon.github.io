MAR OrderHead Experiments

OrderHead training on Masked Autoregressive (MAR) image generation. Bidirectional attention makes MAR naturally order-agnostic, enabling learned ordering without the causal AR paradox.

1. Setup & Baselines

MAR Architecture

MAR-B: ~200M params, bidirectional encoder-decoder with diffusion loss on continuous VAE latents. 256 tokens (16×16), num_iter=64 (cosine AR schedule). MAR-S: ~100M params, same architecture scaled down.

OrderHead

Same architecture as LlamaGen: 4-layer ViT, hidden_dim=256, ~3.5M params. ScoredOrdering with STE binary mask. ViT oracle trained on VQ/VAE tokens with PatchDrop. cls_k=64 (4 clusters of 64 tokens).

3. IN-100: From-Scratch Experiments

Training OrderHead + MAR jointly from random initialization. These experiments test whether the two components can bootstrap each other without a pretrained backbone.

Results (FID-5K, verified from eval logs)

ExperimentFID-5KISscore_stdStatusSource

MAR-S cls_k oracle (400 ep)78.2720.620.012 → recovered to ~1.6Failedeval_in100_oh_s_clsk_5952088.out

Baselines for reference: MAR-S (no OrderHead) = FID-5K ~31.28 (IN-100, online eval during training). MAR-B = FID-5K ~18.87.

From-scratch training failed consistently. The chicken-and-egg problem: at epoch 0, both MAR body and ViT oracle operate on random features, producing meaningless importance scores. OrderHead scores collapse (std → 0.012), and the MAR body trains under random ordering for hundreds of epochs. Even though score_std eventually recovers to ~1.6 by epoch 400, the MAR body has already converged to a suboptimal state. FID is 2.5–3.3x worse than the no-OrderHead baseline.

5. Findings & Lessons Learned

Oracle supervision (cls_k) is the only stable training signal

The cls_k=64 oracle mode evaluates classification accuracy on non-overlapping clusters of 64 tokens each (4 clusters for 256 total). This gives robust per-token importance scores. The alternative saliency_attn mode (CLS→patch attention) produces near-uniform scores (~0.004/patch) and causes OrderHead collapse. All successful experiments use cls_k oracle.

OrderHead works best when initialized from a converged order-agnostic backbone

The only stable OrderHead training happens when the MAR body is already well-trained on random-order data. The pretrained body provides discriminative hidden states for scoring, and can adapt to structured orderings because it was trained on diverse masking patterns. From-scratch training fails because neither component can provide useful signal to the other during early training.

STE-only training is unstable without oracle regularization

Pure STE training causes score_std to grow unboundedly (~8+ by epoch 9), leading to increasingly extreme orderings that push the model out of distribution. Adding oracle MSE as a regularizer (lambda_cls=0.1–1.0) keeps score_std in the 1.0–2.0 range and produces stable FID improvements across epochs.

OrderHead has not improved over the random-order baseline in any experiment

The fundamental result: MAR with random masking (FID=2.30) outperforms MAR with learned ordering (best FID=6.55). This suggests that for bidirectional masked models, the diversity of random masking may be more valuable than the structure of a learned ordering. The OrderHead constrains the model to a specific ordering pattern, which may reduce the effective training distribution.
